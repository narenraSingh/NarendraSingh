Power System FAQs

  • Load Factor is the ratio of actual energy used over a period of time to the maximum possible energy that could have been used in the same time.

  • Diversity Factor is the ratio of the sum of individual maximum demands to the maximum demand of the whole system.

  • Safety Factor is a multiplier applied to design load to account for uncertainties.

  • A Soft Starter is an electronic device that reduces the starting current and torque of an AC induction motor by gradually increasing the voltage during startup.

    ⚡ How it Works: It uses SCRs (Silicon Controlled Rectifiers) to ramp up voltage. Once the motor reaches full speed, a bypass contactor typically closes to save energy.

    🚀 Soft Starter vs VFD (Key Differences):

    Feature Soft Starter VFD
    Speed Control ❌ No (Only Start/Stop) ✅ Yes (Full Control)
    Cost Low High
    Primary Use Reduce Inrush Current Process Automation

    🎯 One-Line Interview Ans: "A soft starter reduces mechanical stress and starting current by ramping up voltage, but unlike a VFD, it cannot control motor speed once the motor is running."

  • An SCR is a power electronic semiconductor device used to control and switch high voltage and high current. It acts like a controlled diode that conducts only when the gate is triggered. Use To control and switch high voltage and high current of Soft-Starters. Soft-Starters use 6 SCRs(2 SCR per phase) SCR behave like a diode with gate I/P that allow control of conduction. It widely use to AC voltage control because firing angle can adjusted to regulate output voltage.

    ⚡ How it Works:

    1. 1. Terminals: It has three terminals: Anode (A), Cathode (K), and Gate (G).
    2. 2. Triggering: A small pulse to the Gate turns the SCR ON.
    3. 3. Commutation: Once ON, it stays ON until the current drops to zero.

    🚀 Why SCRs are used in Soft Starters:

    Soft starters use 6 SCRs (two per phase in back-to-back configuration) to reduce starting voltage. By firing SCRs at different angles of the AC waveform, the starter gradually increases the voltage for smooth motor acceleration.

  • A Direct-On-Line (DOL) starter is the simplest motor Starter, where the motor is directly connected to the full supply voltage through a contactor and overload protection. It consist of connector,overload Relay, and start/stop pushbuttons. It is widely used for industrial motors, pumps, and fans. When You Press Start Button ,Contactor will close and motor is directly connected to the supply Voltage.This Cause Motor Draw high inrush Current(6 to 8 time full load current ) for short time untill motor not reaches its Rated Speed.

    1. Main Components of a DOL Starter:

    1. 1. MCB / MCCB: Protects against short-circuits and isolates the power supply.
    2. 2. Contactor: An electrically controlled switch that connects the motor to power.
    3. 3. Overload Relay (OLR): Trips the circuit when the motor current exceeds its rated value.
    4. 4. Control Elements: Start Push Button (NO), Stop Push Button (NC), and Auxiliary Contacts for the holding circuit.

    2. Starting Characteristics (Current & Torque):

    The major disadvantage of DOL is the high inrush current. The formula for starting current is:

    $$I_{start} = 6 \text{ to } 8 \times I_{rated}$$

    Starting torque is high because it is proportional to the square of the voltage: $$T \propto V^2$$. This makes DOL ideal for pumps, compressors, and crushers.

    3. Selection Guide (DOL vs. Star-Delta vs. VFD):

    Power Limit Starter Type Voltage Applied
    Up to 5 HP DOL Starter Full Voltage
    5 – 15 HP Star-Delta Starter Reduced (1/√3)
    Above 15 HP Soft Starter / VFD Controlled Ramp

    4. Selection Example (15 kW Motor):

    1. 1. Current Calculation: $$I = \frac{P}{\sqrt{3} \times V \times PF \times \eta}$$
    2. 2. Assume: 415V, 0.85 PF, 0.9 Efficiency → $I \approx 28\text{ A}$.
    3. 3. Selection: Select a 32A or 40A Contactor to handle the load safely.

    5. Protective Functions:

    • ⭐ Phase Failure Relay: Protects against single-phasing.
    • ⭐ Earth Fault Relay: Protection against leakage current.
    • ⭐ Interlocking: Prevents accidental restarts after power failure (3-Wire Control).

    Interview One-Line Answer: "A DOL starter connects a motor directly to the grid, providing full torque but high inrush current; it is typically used for motors up to 5 HP where the grid can handle the starting surge."

  • A Star–Delta starter is a motor starting technique where the motor initially runs in a star configuration to reduce the starting current, and after a preset time, it switches to a delta configuration to run at full voltage and rated power. Used To reduce starting current of Induction Motor. IM Draw 6-8 time FLC during starting,Star-Delta Reduce by 1/3. Star-Delta Reduce the starting current of IM by starting the motor in star and when Motor acheive his rated speed It shift to delta using Timer and interlock contactors. Each winding now Get full line voltage for normal Operation.

    1. Why Star–Delta is Necessary:

    Starting a motor directly (DOL) causes a high inrush current ($$I_{start} = 6-8 \times I_{rated}$$), leading to voltage dips and mechanical stress. Star–Delta reduces this starting current to approximately:

    $$I_{start} = \frac{1}{3} \times I_{DOL}$$

    2. Main Components of the Panel:

    1. 1. MCCB / MCB: Provides short-circuit protection.
    2. 2. Three Contactors: Main Contactor (KM1), Star Contactor (KM2), and Delta Contactor (KM3).
    3. 3. Timer: Controls the precise switching time from Star to Delta once the motor reaches ~80–90% speed.
    4. 4. Overload Relay: Protects the motor windings from continuous overcurrent.

    3. Working Principle (Step-by-Step):

    1. 1. Star Mode: Main and Star contactors energize. Phase voltage is reduced ($$V_{phase} = \frac{V_{line}}{\sqrt{3}}$$), which significantly lowers the inrush current.
    2. 2. Transition: The timer opens the Star contactor after a few seconds.
    3. 3. Delta Mode: The Delta contactor energizes, and the motor runs at full line voltage for rated power.

    4. Torque and Requirements:

    Since torque is proportional to the square of the voltage ($$T \propto V^2$$), the starting torque is also reduced to $$1/3$$ of its DOL value. This makes it suitable for medium loads like pumps, fans, and compressors.
    Requirement: The motor must have 6 terminals available (U1, V1, W1 and U2, V2, W2).

    5. Interlocking (Critical for Safety):

    To prevent a phase-to-phase short circuit, Electrical and Mechanical Interlocking is used between the Star and Delta contactors to ensure they can never close at the same time.

    Interview One-Line Answer: "A Star–Delta starter reduces the starting current to one-third of the DOL value by starting the motor in a Star configuration and then switching to Delta once the motor is up to speed."

  • A VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) is an electronic device used to control the speed, torque, and direction of an AC motor by varying the frequency and voltage supplied to the motor. 🧠 How a VFD Works (3 Main Stages) 1️.Rectifier Converts AC supply → DC Uses diodes or thyristors 2️⃣ DC Bus (DC Link) Stores DC energy using capacitors Smoothens DC voltage 3️⃣ Inverter Converts DC → Variable AC Uses IGBTs Controls: Frequency (speed) Voltage (torque)

What a VFD Controls

  • Motor speed
  • Starting current (soft start)
  • Torque
  • Direction (Forward / Reverse)
  • Acceleration & deceleration

What is a Power System?

Power systems are the backbone of electrical energy delivery, encompassing generation, transmission, and distribution. A critical component is the transformer, which adjusts voltage levels to meet load demands. Transformer kVA ratings, determined by factors like load type and capacity, ensure efficient power delivery. For example, selecting the right transformer size involves calculating the total kVA needed for residential, commercial, or industrial applications, ensuring safety and reliability in power distribution networks.

What is Automation?

Automation involves using technology to perform tasks with minimal human intervention, enhancing efficiency in industrial processes. Key components include Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) for controlling machinery and Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems for real-time monitoring. By integrating sensors, actuators, and control systems, automation enables precise process control, reduces errors, and optimizes operations in industries like manufacturing and energy.

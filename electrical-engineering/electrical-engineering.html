Power System FAQs

  • Load Factor is the ratio of actual energy used over a period of time to the maximum possible energy that could have been used in the same time.

  • Diversity Factor is the ratio of the sum of individual maximum demands to the maximum demand of the whole system.

  • Safety Factor is a multiplier applied to design load to account for uncertainties.

  • A Soft Starter is an electronic device that reduces the starting current and torque of an AC induction motor by gradually increasing the voltage during startup.

    ⚡ How it Works: It uses SCRs (Silicon Controlled Rectifiers) to ramp up voltage. Once the motor reaches full speed, a bypass contactor typically closes to save energy.

    🚀 Soft Starter vs VFD (Key Differences):

    Feature Soft Starter VFD
    Speed Control ❌ No (Only Start/Stop) ✅ Yes (Full Control)
    Cost Low High
    Primary Use Reduce Inrush Current Process Automation

    🎯 One-Line Interview Ans: "A soft starter reduces mechanical stress and starting current by ramping up voltage, but unlike a VFD, it cannot control motor speed once the motor is running."

  • An SCR is a power electronic semiconductor device used to control and switch high voltage and high current. It acts like a controlled diode that conducts only when the gate is triggered.

    ⚡ How it Works:

    1. 1. Terminals: It has three terminals: Anode (A), Cathode (K), and Gate (G).
    2. 2. Triggering: A small pulse to the Gate turns the SCR ON.
    3. 3. Commutation: Once ON, it stays ON until the current drops to zero.

    🚀 Why SCRs are used in Soft Starters:

    Soft starters use 6 SCRs (two per phase in back-to-back configuration) to reduce starting voltage. By firing SCRs at different angles of the AC waveform, the starter gradually increases the voltage for smooth motor acceleration.

    📊 Device Comparison:

    Device Primary Use Switching Type
    SCR Soft Starters, Rectifiers Unidirectional
    TRIAC Light Dimmers, Small AC loads Bidirectional
    IGBT VFDs, High-speed switching High-frequency

    🎯 Interview Ans: "An SCR is a high-power semiconductor used to control AC voltage by adjusting the firing angle. While Soft Starters use SCRs for voltage ramping, VFDs typically use IGBTs for frequency control."

  • A VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) is an electronic device used to control the speed, torque, and direction of an AC motor by varying the frequency and voltage supplied to the motor. 🧠 How a VFD Works (3 Main Stages) 1️.Rectifier Converts AC supply → DC Uses diodes or thyristors 2️⃣ DC Bus (DC Link) Stores DC energy using capacitors Smoothens DC voltage 3️⃣ Inverter Converts DC → Variable AC Uses IGBTs Controls: Frequency (speed) Voltage (torque)

What a VFD Controls

  • Motor speed
  • Starting current (soft start)
  • Torque
  • Direction (Forward / Reverse)
  • Acceleration & deceleration

What is a Power System?

Power systems are the backbone of electrical energy delivery, encompassing generation, transmission, and distribution. A critical component is the transformer, which adjusts voltage levels to meet load demands. Transformer kVA ratings, determined by factors like load type and capacity, ensure efficient power delivery. For example, selecting the right transformer size involves calculating the total kVA needed for residential, commercial, or industrial applications, ensuring safety and reliability in power distribution networks.

What is Automation?

Automation involves using technology to perform tasks with minimal human intervention, enhancing efficiency in industrial processes. Key components include Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) for controlling machinery and Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems for real-time monitoring. By integrating sensors, actuators, and control systems, automation enables precise process control, reduces errors, and optimizes operations in industries like manufacturing and energy.

Power System FAQs

  • Load Factor is the ratio of actual energy used over a period of time to the maximum possible energy that could have been used in the same time.

  • Diversity Factor is the ratio of the sum of individual maximum demands to the maximum demand of the whole system.

  • Safety Factor is a multiplier applied to design load to account for uncertainties.

What is a Power System?

Power systems are the backbone of electrical energy delivery, encompassing generation, transmission, and distribution. A critical component is the transformer, which adjusts voltage levels to meet load demands. Transformer kVA ratings, determined by factors like load type and capacity, ensure efficient power delivery. For example, selecting the right transformer size involves calculating the total kVA needed for residential, commercial, or industrial applications, ensuring safety and reliability in power distribution networks.

What is Automation?

Automation involves using technology to perform tasks with minimal human intervention, enhancing efficiency in industrial processes. Key components include Programmable Logic Controllers (PLCs) for controlling machinery and Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) systems for real-time monitoring. By integrating sensors, actuators, and control systems, automation enables precise process control, reduces errors, and optimizes operations in industries like manufacturing and energy.
